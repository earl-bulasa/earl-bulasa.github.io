We will answer this question with one simple answer. A tooth extraction will help you be your best self for your date.

The new year is kicking off full speed, and we will soon be going outa on dates!

Being your best self is important. Most of the ladies schedule facial treatments and dress fitting days early for dates. On one hand, most men are working on the restaurant bookings, chocolate shopping, and flower picking.

You are not your best self when you are suffering from toothaches and even headaches. If you have an impacted wisdom tooth then you need a wisdom tooth extraction. Another cause of extraction is if you have a tooth suffering from decay. Whatever it is, a tooth extraction procedure helps remove the things that are bringing you down.

When do I need a tooth extraction?

There are three main reasons why a tooth extraction in Toronto, Ontario happens.

  1. A tooth badly damaged by decay will need extraction when dental restorations can no longer fix it. Sometimes the extent of the damage is too big for a simple filling. In order to protect the other teeth, a tooth extraction happens.
    If this doesn’t convince you, then you have to understand that tooth decay leads to bad breath. Nobody wants a date with bad breath.
  2. An impacted wisdom tooth needs to be extracted because this can cause pain on your other teeth. You’ll not be able to focus on your tasks, or on your date if something is pressing against your other teeth. Other than toothaches, a wisdom tooth extraction also lessens headaches, and other orofacial pain.
  3. Your dentist also does tooth pulling is to make room in the mouth for the other tooth to align properly. This is usually done in connection with orthodontic treatments.

How long does it take for my gums to heal after a tooth extraction?

Recovery after a tooth extraction lasts between seven to ten days. However, this will depend on the location and the type of extraction. One amazing thing about the body is that it heals by itself. What you can do during this time is to help speed up the healing time.

Tooth extraction aftercare

  • It is important to take the prescribed painkillers by your dentist. This helps to relieve any minor discomfort that you may feel after the procedure.
  • Do not remove the initial gauze pad placed. Leave it there for the first four hours after the procedure.
  • Apply an ice bag on the area. You can leave it there for not more than ten minutes at a time. Leaving an ice pack too long can damage the tissues.
  • Rest for the first 24 hours after the extraction. Avoid any strenuous activities for the meantime that might result in bleeding. Examples of this are heavy lifting and even bending.
  • To help your blood clot where the extraction happened, avoid rinsing, spitting or using a drinking straw. This is only for the first 24 hours.
  • For the time being, avoid using a mouthwash as well during the crucial 24 hours.
  • After the first 24 hours are over, rinse your mouth with a solution made of salt and warm water.
  • Consume only soft foods like mashed potatoes and yogurt. Try to chew on the opposite side of your mouth, away from the extraction area.
  • Avoid smoking, if possible, forever. Smoking interrupts the healing process and might cause inflammation. The disadvantages of smoking greatly outweigh the benefits. For the good of your dental health, we encourage you to quit it once and for all.
  • Schedule a follow-up appointment with your dentist to track the progress of your healing.

If you need a wisdom tooth extraction or any kind of tooth extraction we highly suggest that you do it soon. Schedule an appointment with your dental clinic today.

You are the best version of yourself when you are happy and you feel good about yourself. When you feel good about yourself, you are more confident in dates.

    Yes, you need it. An overall healthy body is only achievable by not taking your dental health for granted. Dental cleaning or oral prophylaxis is one of the vital aspects of having a healthy mouth. Don’t let anyone tell you otherwise. Many people settle for just regular brushing and flossing every day. They think that this is only what it takes to achieve good oral health. During an oral prophylaxis, the dentist cleans the areas in the mouth that daily brushing and flossing do not reach.

    Your Toronto dentist advises that you undergo a dental cleaning procedure at least twice a year. Do this every six months.

     

    DENTOPHOBIA: A HINDERING FACTOR TO A REGULAR DENTAL CLEANING

    Patients suffering from dentophobia are afraid of going to the dentist. There are many elements that a person suffering from dentophobia might fear. These include some of the following:

    • The dentist.
    • Anticipated pain from the procedure.
    • Gagging feeling.
    • Numbing of the mouth. This fear is common among patients who have experienced difficulty in breathing.
    • Others fear the sound of the dental tools used in the procedure of a professional dental cleaning.
    • Needle phobia or being afraid of injections.

    When you feel that this is a serious problem and is affecting your oral health, here are a few things that you can try.

    • Use breathing exercises to relax. Inhaling and exhaling help ease your anxiety and stress. Deep breathing also improves digestion.
    • There is a significant advancement in dental medicine and technology today. Through the aid of sedation dentistry, as advised by your dentist, you can feel more relaxed and at ease.
    • It is also vital that you communicate with your dentist if you have dental fear and anxiety. A two-way conversation with your dentist will help you understand the procedure. This might help ease your fear. Open up with him on what you are feeling because he can help you through it.
    • If the option above does not work for you, then maybe it is time to change your dentist. Find one that you trust and you feel comfortable with.
    • Regular checkups and cleanings will make you more comfortable inside a dental office. Do not miss your regular twice a year dental appointment.

    BENEFITS OF A DENTAL CLEANING

    Dental cleaning has many benefits to help you achieve good dental health.

    • Brighter and whiter teeth are one of the aesthetic benefits of professional teeth cleaning. It removes stains that discolor your teeth.
    • A dental cleaning procedure detects early signs of broken dental fillings and fractures in the teeth.
    • Gum disease, if left untreated, leads to early teeth loss. A regular teeth cleaning helps prevent the advancement of bacteria in the mouth.
    • There is a link between cardiovascular disease and gum disease. Dental cleaning reduces the chance of heart attacks and strokes in the future.
    • Your dentist or hygienist compares your oral health to the result of your previous consultation. Through this, he can monitor specific areas that need that you may need to work on.

    AVERAGE DENTAL CLEANING COST IN TORONTO

    The average teeth cleaning cost in Toronto, Canada ranges between $75 CAD to $200 CAD. The price can reach up to $320 CAD. This price includes dental X-rays and dental exams.

    Most people make resolutions at the start of the new year. Now is the time to that you add this to your list of goals in 2019. Start the year with your first dental cleaning procedure today and return to your dentist before the ends. Are you up for the challenge? You will get healthier and whiter teeth in return, and it just gets better from there.

      You are a likely candidate for gum graft surgery if you’re suffering from gum recession. This treatment corrects receding gums to prevent teeth loss. A periodontist performs it by removing gum tissue from the roof of a patient’s mouth and placing it on the affected area. Proper observance of the gum graft healing stages is important to avoid the recurrence of your gum problems.

      The gum graft surgery procedure

      • First, your periodontist will administer a local anesthesia to numb the affected area. He may also lift existing gum to expose and clean the root of a tooth. In some cases, general anesthesia will be given and the patient will sleep during the entire procedure.
      • Next, he will perform the gum tissue graft process. There are three types that a periodontist will choose from, depending on a patient’s specific needs. These are the connective tissue graft, free gingival graft and pedicle graft. Your dentist in Toronto may prefer to use gum tissue from a tissue bank, instead of extracting it from the roof of a patient’s mouth.
      • Finally, the periodontist will suture the graft and instruct you to rinse with an antibacterial mouthwash. He will advise you to stay on for an hour or two and observe for any issues that may arise after the procedure.

      The gum graft healing stages

      There are two gum graft healing stages that you will undergo for a complete and safe recovery.  

      Gum healing stage 1: healing of the gums

      You will know that healing has begun when the soft tissues are shrinking and there is less swelling. The gum tissues gradually starts to bind to the root surface and nearby bone surface as well.  New vessels will also start to form and the tissues will receive enough blood supply. This healing stage spans from four to six weeks after the surgery.

      Gum healing stage 2: healing of the palate

      The palate takes longer to heal than the gum graft and spans from six to eight weeks following the surgery.  For the first few days you will experience a burning sensation and it will take about 14 days before the discomfort will stop. The healing period may last more than two months if there is swelling or if the tissue removal resulted to an exposed bone.

      Post-Operative Instructions:

      Your surgeon will give you instructions as you go through the post-operative phase. It’s vital that you follow them to prevent unwanted discomfort.

      1. Your periodontist will prescribe antibiotics as well as pain medication for you.
      2. Avoid physical activities like lifting heavy things for at least one day.
      3. If you experience bleeding, place gauze on each side of the site but not directly on top of it.
      4. Your dentist in Toronto will provide a palatal stent to protect your palate as it heals. It minimizes bleeding and keeps the site clear upon the removal of the stitches.
      5. Avoid brushing or rinsing your mouth after the surgery.
      6. Keep your tongue away from the surgical site and avoid brushing it too. Also, keep the dressing in place and refrain from lifting or pulling your lips to inspect the wound.
      7. Use a mouthwash to ward off germs after a few days from the surgery.
      8. Stick to cold and soft food while still recovering from the surgery. These are the ideal food to eat during the recovery period:
      • Yogurt
      • Cottage cheese
      • Ice cream
      • Gelatin
      • Soft-boiled or scrambled eggs
      • Vegetables that are cooked or boiled until it become soft

      It could take a long while for some patients to go through the gum graft healing stages. Other patients take four to eight weeks before their gum tissues heal completely. The healing time will also depend on how much of the tissues were extracted from the roof of the patient’s mouth, as well as its thickness and area.

      You should contact your periodontist immediately if you experience the following after the surgery:

      • Non-stop bleeding even after applying pressure for 20 minutes
      • You experience pain, bruising and swelling that is persistent and discomforting.

      A nearby dentist in Toronto is ready to help you if you have concerns about the gum graft surgery.

        Regular brushing alone is not enough to keep you away from having teeth and gum problems. Your gums may recede or wear away with improper brushing and this exposes the roots of the teeth. This condition can be treated with gum or gingival graft surgery. The gum graft surgery yields benefits resulting in healthy gums and an attractive smile.

        A periodontist specializes in diagnosing and treating periodontal or gum diseases through surgery. It involves taking healthy gum tissues from the roof of a patient’s mouth and placing it on the affected or receded area. The gum grafting treatment corrects gum recession before it’s too late.

        What causes gum recession?

        Most patients may already have symptoms of gum recession; however, they are not fully aware of it. Here are factors that contribute to gum recession.

        • Insufficient oral care
        • Inherited genes
        • Aggressive brushing of teeth
        • Periodontal disease or bacterial gum infection
        • Hormonal changes
        • Excessive smoking
        • Teeth grinding or bruxism
        • Crooked or misaligned teeth

        Types of gum graft surgery

        Periodontists use three different methods of gum graft surgery to match the need of each patient, such as.

        1.Connective tissue grafts.

        In this method, the periodontist removes gum tissue from the roof of the mouth by creating a flap and harvesting tissue from underneath the top layer. The tissue is then stitched into the existing gum tissue and the flap on the roof of the mouth is stitched back.

        2.Free gingival grafts.

        The periodontist recommends this type of surgery to patients with thin gums. In this procedure, the tissue is removed directly from the top layer located at the roof of the mouth. It is then stitched to the affected gum area.

        3.  Pedicle or lateral grafts.

        This gum grafting treatment suits well with patients who have a lot of gum tissues near the receded area or the exposed tooth. The periodontist grafts from the nearby gums and only partially cuts away the tissues. He then stretches the tissues over to cover the exposed tooth and stitches it securely.

        Benefits of gum graft surgery

        • It helps protect the tooth against further recession.
        • It stabilizes and strengthens the gums around the tooth.
        • It decreases sensitivity in the teeth with the replacement of new gums.
        • It restores your smile and self-confidence.

        Know its drawbacks

        Despite the satisfactory level of gum graft surgery among patients, it is also important to know its drawbacks, such as:

        • Swollen and bleeding gums
        • Loosened teeth although this is temporary
        • Sensitive teeth
        • Infection
        • The appearance of spaces between teeth

        Precautions to avoid gum problems

        A gum grafting treatment is a vital solution to periodontal disease. There are necessary precautions to prevent it before it becomes a full-blown problem. These are just some of the precautions that you can do:

        • Maintain a healthy lifestyle by eating well-balanced meals.
        • Quit smoking.
        • Brush your teeth at least twice a day with a fluoride toothpaste.
        • Floss every day.
        • Visit your dentist at least twice a year for dental check-up and cleaning.
        • Schedule an appointment with your periodontist as needed.

        Proper oral care means caring not only for your teeth but your gums as well. A regular dental visit is also needed for professional teeth cleaning. Never hesitate to refer to your periodontist if you’re showing symptoms of gum recession. It can be treated with gum graft therapy to restore the health of your teeth and gums.

          Dentists perform tooth extraction in Toronto to prevent their patients’ teeth succumb to decay. Some causes for tooth extraction include cavities, impacted wisdom tooth and overcrowding. Some dentists recommend removal of some teeth for patients with overcrowding case prior to the orthodontic treatment.

          TWO TYPES OF TOOTH EXTRACTION IN TORONTO

          Dentists examine the tooth first prior to the extraction to determine whether a simple or surgical procedure will be required.

          Simple tooth extraction

          Your dentist begins with administering a sedative which anesthetize the surrounding area near the tooth. You only experience pressure during the procedure. Best dentists in Toronto use an elevator to loosen your tooth. After, they use forceps for removal.

          Surgical tooth extraction

          Before any surgical procedure, practitioners give their patients sedative to calm them. Dentists begin by creating a small incision in your gum. Sometimes, they need to remove the bones left in the socket prior to the tooth extraction in Toronto.

          RECOVERING FROM TOOTH EXTRACTION IN TORONTO

          When dentists speak of recovery, patients think it takes a while before their teeth heal. For patients who went through tooth extraction, recovery period takes at least a week before they feel zero pain.

          Here are the tips from the best dentists in Toronto that patients need to follow for a speedy recovery.

          • Apply cold compress in the area with a 10-minute interval.
          • When pain becomes unbearable, consult your dentist for some medication.
          • Make sure to rest completely for 24 hours before going back to your usual routine.
          • Recovery from tooth extraction in Toronto involves avoiding smoking, and other harmful substances.
          • Drink directly from your mug or cup instead of sipping through a straw. It’s less painful and eco-friendly!
          • Continue your usual dental routine of brushing and flossing; however, avoid the area where your tooth got extracted.
          • Go on a soft food diet. The best dentists in Toronto recommend consuming yogurt and pudding.
          • Refrain from using oral antiseptic after the procedure until the next 24 hours to prevent bleeding. Then, you can rinse your mouth with saline solution the next day as it promotes healing in the area quickly.

          Dentists may not always recommend tooth extraction to their patients in order to preserve their natural teeth. This procedure should be the last option, and patients need to adhere to their dentists’ recommendation for a fast recovery.

            A dental X-ray helps dentists examine a patient’s teeth. It assists dental practitioners in preventing and treating possible mouth problems before these become serious health issues. Teeth and bones absorb the radiation emitted by the X-ray and then it reaches the gums.

            Some dentists from Toronto dental clinic use dental X-rays for the following reasons:

            • Examining cavities
            • Checking teeth roots
            • Assess gum problems and other diseases
            • Observe the growing teeth

            Types of dental X-ray

            Dental X-ray consists of two (2) primary types: intraoral and extraoral.

            Intraoral

            Dentists find this the most common X-ray and more informative about the internal aspect of the mouth. These are the three (3) types of intraoral X-ray.

            Bitewings

            This features the crowns at the rear of the mouth. Patients bite down on a wing-shaped gadget that keeps the film in place. Dentists from Toronto dental clinic use this to examine and look for decay.

            Periapical

            It exhibits the entirety of each tooth. Dentists see the full length of the tooth spanning from its crown to its root.

            Occlusal

            This type of dental X-ray is required among children. Dentists see how their teeth develop in the mouth. One X-ray exhibits either lower or upper jaw and includes the child’s almost full arch of teeth.

            Extraoral

            This shows the entire teeth and provides information on the patient’s jaw. Here are the three (3) types of extraoral X-ray.

            Panoramic

            This type shows the entire mouth through a single X-ray. Dentists from Toronto dental clinic see all the teeth in the upper and lower jaws of their patients. A panoramic X-ray helps dentists check the growth of wisdom teeth.

            Cephalometric projections

            This type aids dentists to know the right orthodontic treatment that their patients need. It shows the entire side of the patient’s head.

            Cone-beam computed tomography

            Dentists require this type of X-ray if the patient needs to undergo a dental implant procedure. This guides the dentists in choosing the implant and location.

            Standard computed tomography

            Dentists require this from patients especially if they need to know the location and size of the dental implants. This emits higher amounts of radiation than the cone-beam CT.

            When patients learn about different types of dental X-ray, it helps them understand what’s happening in their teeth.

              Snoring seems like a minor problem but it conceals other health problems that you may not be aware of. Knowing the best snoring solutions out there helps you resolve this problem.

              Why do you snore?

              When you sleep, muscles at the rear area of the throat relax and close momentarily. Air passes through a smaller hole which quickly causes vibrations. Tissues around the rear area vibrate which results to snoring. Other causes for snoring stem from breathing through the mouth and having a nasal obstruction.

              What are the best snoring solutions?

              According to the Dentists in Toronto, patients can choose from various solutions that fit their lifestyle to resolve snoring problems.

              Change sleeping position

              When you sleep, remember to sleep on your side and not on your back. Lying on your back lets the base of the tongue and your soft palate to crash to the rear area of the throat. Hence, it results to creating vibrations during your sleep. Elevate your head by putting a pillow under your head. Remember this as one of the best snoring solutions at home.
