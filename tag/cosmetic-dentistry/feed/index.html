Everyone wants to have a beautiful smile, full of straight and evenly spaced teeth. A good smile can make a great first impression, but a crooked smile can also have negative consequences for a person’s overall health. Until recently, the only way to correct crooked, crowded or unevenly spaced teeth was with traditional metal braces. However, metal braces  are readily apparent and can make some people feel insecure about their smile. For this reason, if you have crooked teeth, your Toronto dentist may recommend invisalign Toronto.

 

What Is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a program for teeth straightening. At the beginning of the program, your dentist will examine and make a mold of your teeth. Using this mold, a clear, nearly invisible, plastic retainer will be made. You have to wear this plastic retainer neatly all day for two weeks, only taking it out to eat and clean your teeth. After the two weeks are over, your dentist will examine your teeth again and give you new aligners. Depending on how malleable your gums are, this process will gradually shift your teeth into place over the course of six months to two years.

 

Why Should I Use Invisalign?

Invisalign straightens your teeth and, in so doing, can

  • Improve your appearance. Everyone looks better with a beautiful smile.
  • Make you more confident. If you feel insecure about crooked or improperly spaced teeth, invisalign may be just the thing to get you out of your shell.
  • Make your teeth easier to clean. If food is often caught between gaps or certain surfaces are hidden due to crowded teeth, invisalign can fix that.
  • Even fix certain speech impediments. Some people have problems speaking because of their teeth. For example, if you have a slight lisp because of a gap between your two front teeth, Invisalign will close the gap.

    The interest in cosmetic dentistry has increased over the last decade and procedures have become more popular. Cosmetic procedures can range from simple treatment options such as teeth whitening to comprehensive treatments such as implants or total mouth reconstruction surgery. Whether you are contemplating a simple or complex procedure, there are certain considerations that need to be made beforehand. Here are a few.

     

    Have a Consultation First

    Consultations are beneficial and should not be skipped due to time constraints. A dental professional can explain the type of procedure you are going to undergo and answer any questions you may have. Together you can set treatment goals and discuss the steps necessary to ensure it is successful. The dentist can explain the timeline, help you set realistic goals and talk about any possible complications.

     

    Review Previous Procedures

    The dentist should be able to produce before and after pictures of some of his previous work. Ask about cases that are similar to the procedure you are planning. Reputable dentists, will be glad to share their work with you. You may also ask for any references from patients in your community.

     

    Sedation and Technology

    You should feel comfortable about asking the dentist about the types of technology he plans to use. Most dental professionals are using the most recent advancements in technology and should be staying up to date with the latest trends. Also ask about the types of anesthesia that will be used during the procedure.

     

    Understand Your Part of the Costs

    Before you commit to any cosmetic dental procedure, be certain you understand what costs you will be responsible for. Make sure you know what your insurance will and will not pay so you will not what is covered and what you will need to pay out of pocket. You may discuss financing options with the office for procedures that are more expensive.

      Are you looking for a clinic that offers cosmetic dental treatments in the Toronto? It’s important to do a little bit of research when you are looking for a cosmetic dentist. Think about it this way, a dental professional who provides cosmetic dental treatments is going to be performing services that will change your smile and appearance forever. It’s an important decision and you should be able to make it feeling confident and secure.

       

      Where can you find cosmetic dental treatments?

      One of the best ways to find a cosmetic dentist is to do a search on Google. In just a matter of a few seconds you can generate a list of dental professionals in your region. Most dentist’s websites will list the types of services they provide and you can find the one you need in your particular situation. Don’t forget to read through any reviews others have left as these offer a good idea of the type of patient-professional relationship you can look forward to.

       

      How to make the final choice on the best cosmetic dentist for you.

      You can use different criteria for selecting a cosmetic dentist. For many patients, their base is based simply on costs. Cosmetic dental treatments typically cost more than general dentistry services but you can find the most affordable cosmetic dentist and discuss financing options with them.

       

      What types of questions should I ask?

      There are a few key areas to consider when you are trying to find the best cosmetic dental treatments. Here are a few to get you started:

      • Does the dentist pursue continued education?
      • What types of credentials does the dentist have?
      • How much of the dentist’s time is devoted to cosmetic procedures?
      • Can the dentist provide you with “before and after” photos?
      • Does he take time to discuss your concerns?
      • Are you comfortable in their office?

        Straighter teeth can improve your appearance, your self confidence and even your health. Going to your dentist is the right first step if your teeth are crowded, crooked or misaligned. Your dentist might suggest different options, since both Invisalign and Veneers can give you a straighter smile. Your dentist can help you make the decision, but it also helps to know something about the choices yourself. Then, you can ask better questions and feel surer of your decision.

         

        Consider Your Teeth-Straightening Goals

        Think about why you want your teeth straightened. Is it all about appearance for you? If so, either option can be a good choice. Invisalign makes your teeth look straighter, but so can veneers. An advantage of veneersis that they hide stains and discolorations that Invisalign won’t, which is why many people choose to have Invisalign followed by veneers.

        Do you want to have better oral health? Dental veneers can help you take pride in your appearance and thus inspire you to take better care of your teeth. However, veneers require irreversible treatment as the front surface of your tooth’s enamel is removed. Veneers don’t actually make your teeth straighter; they just look that way. Invisalign, on the other hand, can improve your oral health by giving you straighter teeth that are easier to care for and clean.

         

        Recognize What Veneers and Invisalign Can Correct

        Veneers correct visual imperfections such as slightly crooked front teeth. Invisalign can correct deeper problems like crowding, bite issues and crooked teeth. As orthopedist Janet Stoess-Allen explains, the best use of veneers is to cover healthy teeth for cosmetic reasons. When the teeth, especially the bottom teeth, are crowded, orthopedic treatment is the best first step. Invisalign is a true orthopedic treatment, while veneersare primarily cosmetic.

         

        Know Your Time Investment

        How long are you willing to wait to have more beautiful teeth? Your dentist can prepare your teeth and install the veneers in two weeks or less. Invisalign is an orthodontic treatment that usually takes an average of 12 months to completely straighten your teeth.

         

        Understand Life After Treatment

        After having cosmetic orthodontic treatment with Invisalign, your teeth are in healthy positions. Otherwise, they are just as durable and hassle-free as they ever were. Veneers may require you to make certain lifestyle changes. You will need to avoid red wine, smoking, caffeine and other substances that can stain or discolor the veneers. You will also have to be more careful not to chip or crack the veneers than you would after having only Invisalign.

         

        Compare Costs

        The costs for veneers can be as high as $2000 CAD per tooth. The trays for Invisalign may cost more, depending on how many veneers you need. However, when you consider how long each treatment will last, Invisalignactually may be cheaper. Veneers have to be replaced every 10 to 15 years. The entire Invisalign treatment may never have to be done again, especially if you keep going to your dentist so they can correct minor issues before they become major. Wearing a retainer can also eliminate the need to have Invisalign redone.

        So, what is right for you? It may be different from what is right for someone else. Consider all the factors and discuss the options with your dentist. When you do, you can make the right decision based on your teeth and your preferences.
