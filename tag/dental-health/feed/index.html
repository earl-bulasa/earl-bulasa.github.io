Is your bad breath isolating you? Is it telling you that you need to see a doctor as well as a dentist? Stop hiding and treat your bad breath.

 

Halitosis (bad breath)

We all have bad breath from time to time depending on the foods we eat; however, persistent bad breath (known as halitosis) can be treated. Halitosis remedies in Toronto start with first finding the reason for your persistent bad breath.

 

Some of the reasons you may have halitosis are:

  • Constant dry mouth due to medications
  • Illnesses such as diabetes, liver problems, or kidney disease
  • Sinus infection
  • Gastrointestinal issues
  • Lung infection
  • Poor dental hygiene
  • Foods you eat
  • Gingivitis
  • Yeast infections of the mouth
  • Tooth decay
  • Infection such as abscess

Some of the infections can be treated with antibiotics other ailments you will need your primary doctor to work with your dentist and of course, you need to do your part.

 

You can help with prevention

You can help with good oral hygiene and watch what you eat such as onions, garlic, and other foods that cause breath odor. Eating a well-balanced diet, drinking plenty of water, and getting exercise is important to prevent not only bad breath but tooth decay and gingivitis. Drinking water after you eat helps wash the food from between your teeth and along your gums out so it will not cause decay or gingivitis. Having any decay taken care of as soon as possible also will help prevent bad breath. Dr. Amelia Chan will examine your mouth, teeth, and tongue to find out what is causing your halitosis. She will then recommend treatments that will stop your bad breath.

 

These treatments may include:

  • Something to help keep the mouth moist especially if your dry mouth is from a medication
  • Proper oral hygiene
  • Seeing your doctor to make sure you are healthy overall

    Depending on how much of your gum has receded, your dentist may suggest doing one of two things. First, if the recession of gum is not too bad, the dentist may suggest you work on preventing further recession. Second, however, if the gum recession is large then the dentist will suggest you have a gum graft.

     

    Cost of gum graft

    Alloderm gum graft cost varies on the size or number of teeth in which a gum graft is needed, however, a typical gum graft for one tooth or small area runs between $600.00 to $1200.00. With contouring it will cost more, running between $1,000-$3,000 per area.

     

    How is gum grafting done

    Gum grafting typically takes tissue from the roof (or palate) of the mouth and places it where the graft is going to be done.

    The difference in the Alloderm gum graft is that the tissue is not removed from your palate but instead uses AlloDerm Regenerative Tissue Matrix.

    The regenerative tissue matrix is placed in the area where the graft will be done.

     

    Why have a gum graft?

    A gum graft helps maintain your natural teeth and keeps your mouth healthy. When caught early a gum graft may only be needed on a small section or one tooth in order to save your tooth and keep it healthy.

     

    Prevention is best

    Prevention is the best treatment and plan. Prevention starts with proper oral hygiene, brushing properly twice a day and flossing properly. This needs to be followed up with biannual visits to your Toronto Ontario Dentist for checkups and an annual cleaning which will remove any tartar or plaque that can cause bacteria growth or decay. Also, during a cleaning, the hygienist will be able to find any place where there is a pocket between the gum and tooth which can be an alert to gum disease that could lead to a gum graft.

      Have you noticed a red or swollen gums problem with your mouth? Your gums may be trying to tell you something.

       

      Signs of oral disease and infection

      You should keep alert and check your gums regularly between dentist visits.

       

      Here are some signs to watch for:

      • Redness
      • Puffiness or swelling
      • Bleeding
      • Shrinking of the gums

      The signs above are signs of periodontal disease or Gingivitis (the very early stages). Gingivitis can be reversed with periodontal therapy. Severe periodontal disease probably will not be reversible, however. So your gums can tell you if you are developing periodontal disease but what else can your gums tell you.

       

      Other health issues your gums may tell you about

      There are some other health issues that your gums may be able to alert your dentist to during a checkup.

       

      Some of these issues are:

      • Diabetes or pre-diabetes
      • Cardiovascular disease, stroke, or heart attack
      • Autoimmune diseases such as celiac, lupus, RA, or others
      • Sleep apnea which can cause a clenching and grinding in your sleep to open airways so you can breathe
      • Pregnancy as morning sickness can be rough on your teeth and poor gum and teeth health can lead to premature births
      • Eating disorders or other dietary issues
      • Possible kidney issues
      • HPV, HIV, or AIDS
      • Osteoporosis can lead to tooth loss
      • Anemia (lack of iron and red blood cells)

      As you can see it is important to take care of your gums and teeth. Paying close attention to what they are telling you about not only your oral health but your overall health as well can actually save your life and some money.

       

      What you can do help your gums

      Most dentists including Toronto dental office suggest that you eat a healthy and balanced diet, drink plenty of water, and exercise to help keep your mouth and gums healthy.

        How can sedation dentistry in Toronto help you overcome your fear of the dentist or procedures? To answer this first we must look at the safety of sedation dentistry.

         

        Safety and types of sedation

        In order to understand how safe sedation dentistry is you need to know that only trained and licensed sedation dentists can practice sedation dentistry so not all dentists can do sedation. Before you are given any sedation with sedation dentistry a detailed medical history will be taken and you will need to provide a list of any medications you are currently taking and why.         • Oral in which pills or liquid such as valium to help you relax will be used
        • Inhalation where you will be giving nitrous oxide better known as laughing gas
        • IV moderate sedation where you will be given a sedative through a vein
        • General anesthesia which is where you are given medication that relaxes you and puts you to sleep

        With any of these, you will also generally be given a local anesthesia of Novocain to numb the area of the mouth that is being worked on.

         

        How sedation helps you

        Sedation can provide help to those who are extremely fearful of going to the dentist or are anxious when going to the dentist to have procedures done. It also helps young children receive proper dental care without being further traumatized by the dentist because the sedation helps relieve the fear and anxiousness that the child has. In effect, it is a great tool for both children and adults that allow the patient to have dental care procedures done in order for your Toronto Ontario Dentist to keep their mouth and teeth health.

          What is Holistic Dentistry?

          Holistic dentistry is a dental practice that takes into account the emotional and physical health of an individual. A Holistic dentist Toronto utilizes natural therapies that diagnose, curb, and treat oral health problems.

          Dentists practicing on a holistic level not only focuses on the gums and teeth, but also on the entire body. They offer recommendations or programs that go beyond just practicing daily oral hygiene. They place emphasis on healthy nutrition, moderate consumption of alcohol, and smoking cessation.

           

          Chronic Health Concerns

          The perspective of dentists taking on a holistic approach may differ. For instance, some of them may be against the use of fluorides since they feel it’s not capable of curbing tooth decay. In addition, they feel fluoride can trigger chronic health problems such as degenerative bone disease and cancer.

          A majority of holistic dentists aren’t in favor of performing root canals. They feel that making use of sterilizing agents could further trap bacteria in the canal, which can lead to long-term health problems.

           

          Avoiding Metals

          Holistic dentists prefer to not to utilize nickel, amalgam, or other metallic elements in filling cavities. They’d rather settle for a composite resin. The use of this material is widely seen in dentists who don’t even think of themselves as holistic. Holistic dentists are likely to advise patients to have their amalgam fillings removed have them replaced with natural and healthier components.

           

          Less Radiation

          Making use of digital x-rays, which limits radiation exposure to patients, happens to be part of holistic dentistry. Some conventional dentists today are utilizing this equipment to limit a patient’s exposure to radiation.

           

          Ask Questions

          Whether one is exposed to a dentist with a Downtown dental holistic or conventional approach, don’t hesitate to inquire about the materials being utilized in all their dental procedures, how they dispose their dental waste, and their means of treatment.

            Regular dental checkups are considered important since they ensure one’s gums and teeth remain healthy. It’s imperative to have a Dental Checkup Toronto regularly once every several months or as advised by the dentist.

             

            Checkup and Cleaning

            There are essentially two parts being done on a typical dental visit: checkup and cleaning.

            X-rays may be utilized to examine the presence of cavities in between the teeth. The exam also involves checking for any tartar and/or plaque buildup. Plaque appears as a sticky and transparent layer of bacteria. If not properly removed, it can later solidify to form tartar.

            The next process involves the checking of gums. This is typically done through the use of a tool that measures the depth of spaces in between the gums and teeth. When the gums are healthy, the spaces will appear hollow. But when there’s gum disease, the spaces will appear deep.

            The checkup includes thorough examination of the head, neck, face, throat, and tongue. The purpose of this is to observe for any abnormalities such as redness, swelling, or other cancer signs.

             

            Scaling

            Teeth will also require cleaning for every dental visit. While brushing and flossing can clean the plaque, removing tartar isn’t something that can be done at home. During the cleaning process, the dentist will make use of tools to effectively remove tartar buildup. This procedure is known as scaling.

            Once the teeth are scaled, they may require polishing. This process erases surface stains on the teeth. The dentist will utilize floss to ensure areas in between the teeth are squeaky clean.

             

            Dental Visits and Cleaning

            Plaque is constantly being formed on the teeth, but it can be managed by brushing and flossing daily. To ensure the gums and teeth are properly cleaned, visit Dr. Amelia Chan regularly or as recommended.

              Everyone wants to have a beautiful smile, full of straight and evenly spaced teeth. A good smile can make a great first impression, but a crooked smile can also have negative consequences for a person’s overall health. Until recently, the only way to correct crooked, crowded or unevenly spaced teeth was with traditional metal braces. However, metal braces  are readily apparent and can make some people feel insecure about their smile. For this reason, if you have crooked teeth, your Toronto dentist may recommend invisalign Toronto.

               

              What Is Invisalign?

              Invisalign is a program for teeth straightening. At the beginning of the program, your dentist will examine and make a mold of your teeth. Using this mold, a clear, nearly invisible, plastic retainer will be made. You have to wear this plastic retainer neatly all day for two weeks, only taking it out to eat and clean your teeth. After the two weeks are over, your dentist will examine your teeth again and give you new aligners. Depending on how malleable your gums are, this process will gradually shift your teeth into place over the course of six months to two years.

               

              Why Should I Use Invisalign?

              Invisalign straightens your teeth and, in so doing, can

              • Improve your appearance. Everyone looks better with a beautiful smile.
              • Make you more confident. If you feel insecure about crooked or improperly spaced teeth, invisalign may be just the thing to get you out of your shell.
              • Make your teeth easier to clean. If food is often caught between gaps or certain surfaces are hidden due to crowded teeth, invisalign can fix that.
              • Even fix certain speech impediments. Some people have problems speaking because of their teeth. For example, if you have a slight lisp because of a gap between your two front teeth, Invisalign will close the gap.

                A solution for severe gum recession is gum grafting. The process typically involves removing some tissue from the palate of the mouth, which can be quite painful. An alternative to this, that achieves the same results, is AlloDerm gum grafting in Toronto. The procedure is completely safe and leads to a realistic appearance. Here are some information you might need to know about AlloDerm gum grafting.

                 

                How Is AlloDerm Gum Grafting Different?

                AlloDerm is made from donated human skin tissue. All donors are tested for transmissible diseases donation before they give any tissue. The donated skin undergoes minimal processing to keep it as close as possible to natural tissue. Just the epidermis and cells that can lead to tissue rejection (and therefore graft failure) are removed.

                With AlloDerm gum grafting, there is no need to remove any of your living tissue. This eliminates an additional surgical site in your mouth and allows your dentist to repair all areas of the gums that are recessed in a single sitting. This reduces trips to the dentist, discomfort, and cost. It also means you only need to recover from surgery once.

                 

                Why Is Gum Grafting Necessary?

                Once your gums start to recede, they are unable to grow back on their own. This leads to the exposure of the root of the tooth, which can become worn-down or suffer abrasions. You will also notice that your teeth are more sensitive to temperature. Plus, you will have a higher risk of decay, which may result in cavities.

                In the worse cases, gum recession can lead to bone loss, which eventually causes tooth loss. When bone levels drop too low, it is no longer possible to receive sufficient gum grafting. Therefore, if your dentist recommends AlloDerm gum grafting, it is important to receive the surgery as soon as possible.

                  Lumineers are an alternative to the traditional dental veneers that improve the appearance of stained, damaged, or broken teeth. They are also suitable for fixing minor misalignments, especially for patients who would rather not spend a long time with braces. If you want to fix multiple problems without extensive work, Lumineers teeth Toronto are an ideal solution for you.

                   

                  The Process for Receiving Lumineers

                  To receive Lumineers, you will need to visit the clinic just two times after your initial consultation. At your first appointment, your dentist will take an impression and photo of all of your teeth and send these to a Lumineers-authorized laboratory. No other preparation is necessary on this first visit.

                  You will return to the clinic around two weeks later when your Lumineers are ready. Your dentist will begin by setting the Lumineers on your teeth to check the fit. If everything looks good, your dentist will etch your teeth before bonding the Lumineers in place, tooth by tooth. Finally, your dentist will check your bite to ensure that you will be able to eat and speak normally without risking damage to the Lumineers.

                   

                  How Do Lumineers Differ from Traditional Veneers?

                  Like traditional veneers, Lumineers are a permanent solution that involves improving the appearance of your teeth. Traditional veneers, however, require the removal of a significant amount of tooth surface. As Lumineers are the thickness of a contact lens, simply etching the surface is enough. This leads to a more natural look, eliminates the need for temporary veneers, and makes the procedure pain-free, which means no anesthesia is necessary.

                  Another difference between traditional veneers and Lumineers is that, whereas all general dentists are trained to fit veneers, only some can offer Lumineers. Any dental professional who offers you this service will be a Certified Lumineers Dentist.

                    Whether or not you are a tea or coffee lover, you may have noticed dark spots forming on your teeth. They are not as white as they used to be. Teeth whitening can be the perfect solution for those whose teeth are stained or have dulled over time. It can be essential for restoring your confidence and great smile.

                     

                    Benefits of Tooth Whitening Treatments

                    A beautiful smile that you are proud of is more than just a case of aesthetic appearance. When teeth become yellowed, people can feel self-conscious and ashamed. They may start to put their hand up to hide their teeth when they speak or laugh. Having white teeth can boost your confidence levels and improve your self-esteem. Having an unattractive smile can make you feel like you cannot pursue all your professional goals. Whiter teeth can give you the confidence boost necessary to take on that corporate job you’ve always dreamed of.

                     

                    What is Teeth Whitening Treatment Like

                    In most cases, teeth whitening sessions will last about an hour and your teeth will be about 10 times whiter. Your teeth are sectioned off to ensure the whitening agents do not come in contact with any other tissues. Then a whitening gel will be applied to the teeth. The specially formulated gel will break down the molecules in the stain. A special light will be used on the teeth to intensify the process.

                     

                    Why Pursue Teeth Whitening Treatments

                    Teeth whitening is a simple and painless way to brighten your smile. It can be done by a cosmetic or general dentist. It only takes one appointment to restore your smile. Although home kits are available, however they are far less effective than the professional teeth whitening offered by a dental professional.
