Investing in the right solution is important if you have a missing tooth. If you want to invest in something that is sturdy and will last you a long time. Dental implants are a popular solution nowadays because they function and look like regular teeth.

What are the disadvantages of not replacing a missing tooth?

  • Not replace your missing tooth is a popular cause of jaw bone loss. The roots of a tooth stimulate and support the bone of your jaws. When you lose a tooth, the bone is no longer stimulated, therefore, it can collapse. Titanium dental implants are surgically inserted in the jaw bone to support it and the tooth replacement. This process is also called osseointegration.
  • Missing a tooth can cause difficulty in chewing. This is especially hard if you are missing one tooth in front. Back teeth do all the heavy chewing of meat and other foods, missing them can affect how you eat.
  • As time goes by, teeth shifts because they are trying to fill in the space left by a missing tooth. The shifting of teeth creates more space which might cause other teeth to shift as well. Teeth movement affects your entire bite.
  • A change in your bite can lead to problems like headaches, muscle pain, wearing of the teeth, and even TMJD.
  • Teeth also support the shape of your face. Not replacing a missing tooth can cause your face to appear aged.
  • You can also suffer some difficulty in speaking if you have a missing tooth.

Why are dental implants a good choice?

Dental implants in Toronto Ontario has many benefits. The following are what makes them a good choice in replacing a missing tooth.

  • They help improve your appearance. Implants look like your natural teeth. No one will know that they are simply a tooth replacement. They also feel like natural teeth since they have a screw that fuses with the bone to make them permanent.

  • With dental implants, you are also able to speak more properly and clearly. Dentures that do not fit you properly can slip within your mouth. This can cause a mumble or slur in your words. Implants remove the worry that they might slip anytime.
  • Implants do not only improve speaking but also makes eating easier. Dentures that slide out of place can make chewing meat difficult. With implants, you chew food with the comfort of regular teeth.
  • As opposed to removable dentures, titanium dental implants gives you more comfort. The reason for this is because they are like your natural teeth. You do not need to remove and give them special cleaning every night.
  • Oral health greatly improves with implants. It doesn’t need reduction of other teeth like with dental bridges. Your other natural teeth are intact, which improves their longevity. Implants also make cleaning of the teeth easier, improving your oral hygiene.
  • Dental implants are sure to last many years with good oral hygiene and proper care.
  • Improve your self-esteem with implants. You don’t have to worry about your tooth replacement falling apart. It restores normal chewing patterns and when you smile, no one will know the difference.

Invest in the right treatment when replacing a missing tooth. This is because you will be wearing them for a long time in your life. Titanium dental implants are a great choice. It has many benefits that will make your life easier. Improve your confidence in life by replacing a missing tooth, you will not regret it.

    Dental implants are by far the best way to replace missing teeth. Not only do they replace the function of the crown of the tooth, but they replace the function of the root as well. Other tooth replacements, such as partial dentures or bridges function well for chewing, speaking, and looking good when you speak and smile.  But a dental implant can strengthen and reinforce the jawbone. A dental implant can help align your teeth. Most importantly, a dental implant is firmly implanted in the jaw. So what’s the catch? What’s the dental implant cost  Toronto?

    There’s no denying it. Dental implants are expensive. For this reason, your Toronto dentist will likely recommend a bridge or denture if you are missing a single tooth. However, because a dental implant is firmly implanted in the jaw, the are often used to make a stable location to attach bridges or dentures.

     

    Procedure

    If your tooth has been lost recently, either naturally or by an extraction, you may be able to get the implant right away. However, if it’s been a while since you lost your tooth, you may need a bone graft to strengthen the jawbone first. If your jawbone is reasonably strong, you are ready to get your dental implant.

    A dental implant is made of two parts. The first part is either a post firmly implanted in the jaw, in the case of an endosteal implant, or a metal frame fitted under the gums, in the case of subperiosteal implants. The second is a crown, custom made and fitted to have the same appearance and function as your natural tooth.

        Your dentist will recommend filling the gap through a tooth replacement shortly after a tooth extraction to avoid other teeth from drifting into the space. There are a number of options for tooth replacement in Toronto, each with its own advantages and disadvantages.

         

        Dental Implant

        Dental implants are the most popular option for tooth replacement, being that they are the most natural-looking, durable, and stable. As the implant is inserted into the jawbone, it stimulates bone growth and avoids bone loss. Although implants have high upfront costs, they can last a lifetime, meaning they tend to offer a good value for money.

         

        Fixed Bridge

        The second most common option, a fixed bridge takes much less time to fit than an implant and there is no need for surgery. The biggest disadvantage is that the procedure requires some destruction of healthy teeth — to fit the bridge, your dentist will need to grind down your teeth on either side of the gap.

         

        Removable Partial Dentures

        Removable partial dentures replace a number of teeth. You have the option to attach the removable teeth using clasps or connectors. The latter appear more natural but are more expensive. When you first receive your partial dentures, they will likely feel uncomfortable, but you will become accustomed to the sensation over time. You will also need to dedicate some practice to removing the dentures at night and inserting them in the morning.

         

        Complete Dentures

        If you are missing all your teeth, you may like to opt for complete dentures. You can receive immediate dentures, made before your dentist removes your teeth, to eliminate the waiting period after extraction. However, the dentures will likely need many adjustments as your mouth heals and changes shape. This makes immediate dentures a good temporary solution but conventional dentures a better permanent option.
