Everyone wants to have a beautiful smile, full of straight and evenly spaced teeth. A good smile can make a great first impression, but a crooked smile can also have negative consequences for a person’s overall health. Until recently, the only way to correct crooked, crowded or unevenly spaced teeth was with traditional metal braces. However, metal braces  are readily apparent and can make some people feel insecure about their smile. For this reason, if you have crooked teeth, your Toronto dentist may recommend invisalign Toronto.

 

What Is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a program for teeth straightening. At the beginning of the program, your dentist will examine and make a mold of your teeth. Using this mold, a clear, nearly invisible, plastic retainer will be made. You have to wear this plastic retainer neatly all day for two weeks, only taking it out to eat and clean your teeth. After the two weeks are over, your dentist will examine your teeth again and give you new aligners. Depending on how malleable your gums are, this process will gradually shift your teeth into place over the course of six months to two years.

 

Why Should I Use Invisalign?

Invisalign straightens your teeth and, in so doing, can

  • Improve your appearance. Everyone looks better with a beautiful smile.
  • Make you more confident. If you feel insecure about crooked or improperly spaced teeth, invisalign may be just the thing to get you out of your shell.
  • Make your teeth easier to clean. If food is often caught between gaps or certain surfaces are hidden due to crowded teeth, invisalign can fix that.
  • Even fix certain speech impediments. Some people have problems speaking because of their teeth. For example, if you have a slight lisp because of a gap between your two front teeth, Invisalign will close the gap.

    Many adults never straightened their teeth, often due to worries about discomfort or embarrassment of having braces. Invisalign brings a new way for adults and children alike to correct the alignment of their teeth. Before deciding if this is the right treatment for you, consider the Invisalign pros and cons Toronto.

     

    Pros

    They’re invisible

    The main reason for the popularity of Invisalign is that it is unlikely anyone will know you are using aligners. They are made from clear plastic, free from metal brackets and bands.

     

    You can eat whatever you want

    As you remove aligners when you eat, there are no restrictions on your diet.

     

    They help you maintain good dental hygiene.

    You also remove aligners when you brush your teeth, making it easier to access every area of your mouth and ensure no food remnants remain.

     

    They’re comfortable

    Aligners should have a smooth surface — nothing to rub against the gums or sides of your mouth. If you find any rough edges, return to your dentist for an adjustment.

     

    They’re affordable

    Invisalign costs about the same as traditional metal brackets.

     

    Cons

    You need to adhere closely to your dentist’s guidelines.

    Invisalign only works if you wear aligners for an average of 22 hours per day. You need to commit to keeping to the rules; otherwise, the treatment will fail.

     

    You need to remove them before eating

    Although this is an advantage for some people, it is a disadvantage for others. Depending on your lifestyle, you may find it inconvenient to remove aligners before you eat or drink anything other than water. To make matters worse, you also need to clean your teeth before reinserting aligners.

     

    They are unsuitable for severe misalignment

    Aligners correct many issues, but severe misalignment may take too long to fix or you may fail to see the results you wanted.

      It’s a big decision to choose an orthodontic clinic for your family. These dental professionals hold the key to your family’s smile and oral health for years to come. There are a few steps that can help you find the best orthodontics clinic in Toronto.

       

      How do I know if I’m choosing an orthodontic specialist?

      Most clinics will have a prominently displayed logo, stationary or business cards that states they are certified as orthodontic specialists. You can also call or visit the office and ask their receptionist or the dentist for such verification.

       

      Does the clinic observe early treatment?

      It is advised for children to see an orthodontist specialist as early as seven years old. A general dentist or pediatrician can make the recommendation. Roughly half of all orthodontists do not begin treatment until a patient has their adult teeth. However, some problems are treatable early on while the child is still growing. Crowded teeth, severe mouth breathing, thumb or finger sucking, an overbite and a narrow upper jaw can, and should, be solved early for best results.

       

      Does the clinic use the latest advanced technology?

      A progressive office will be up to date when it comes to using the latest technology. For instance, a paperless office is going to offer better communication and greater patient access so you can access your personal dental information. It’s also important that the orthodontist you choose use advanced options in braces. Newer choices help make treatment faster and easier as well as causing less discomfort than traditional treatment options. The latest treatments produce better outcomes and mean less time spent in the orthodontist clinic.

       

      Is there a comprehensive fee?

      Most orthodontists now offer patients a comprehensive fee covering appointments, x-rays, retainers, photos, appliances and checkups until treatment is completed.

        You may have already looked into getting Invisalign in downtown Toronto, but maybe you are wondering just how the treatment process works. It’s a step-by-step process starting with a professional consultation. Understanding how the process is going to play out will help you prepare for the treatment and know what you should expect along the way.

         

        Step 1 – Talk to Your Provider about Invisalign in downtown Toronto

        Once you have selected the Invisalign provider in Toronto, you can schedule a consultation with the dentist who will be treating you. The discussion between you and the dentist will be confidential and together you will talk about your personal goals and the treatment process. You can ask him/her any questions you may have. You might want to ask about the cost, insurance, how long the process may take or anything else that you need an answer to. The dentist will be able to address all your concerns.

         

        Step 2 – A Customized Plan will be Developed

        The dentist will take x-rays and make impressions of your teeth. The impression will be used to make the aligners you will be using. Your dentist will create a customized treatment plan including how your teeth are going to move and approximately how long the treatment is going to take. Each case is unique to the individual, but treatment usually takes about a year.

         

        Step 3 – Receive Your Aligners

        You will be given a series of custom-made aligners that have been created specifically to fit you. They are clear, smooth, and comfortable to wear. They are not going to irritate your cheeks and gums like traditional braces tend to do. You will wear the aligners during the day and remove them to eat and take care of your teeth.

         

        Step 4 – Wearing Your Aligners

        About every week you will retire a pair of aligners and take out a new one. Each time you change aligners, you are completing a stage of your treatment and starting a new one. You will also have regular checkups with the Invisalign downtown Toronto dentist to ensure you are making proper progress. Appointments are usually scheduled about every six weeks. It is best to wear the aligners for 20 to 22 hours every day. You should start to notice the changes in your smile as your teeth move into proper positioning.

         

        Step 5 – Enjoy Your Smile

        After you’ve worn all of the aligners prescribed by the dentist, you will be at the end of your treatment. Now, you can simply enjoy your new smile as you go about your days. You will want to take good care of your smile by practicing good oral hygiene habits and wearing a mouth guard when participating in rigorous activities.

        These are the basic steps for getting Invisalign treatments in downtown Toronto. The dental professionalswill be there to ensure you make the progress you desire and to answer any questions you may have along the way.
