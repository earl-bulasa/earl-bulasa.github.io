The number of dentists in Toronto has been increasing in recent years. According to a survey published by the Canadian Dental Association, the increase from 2000 to 2010 amounted over 35% in Canada. The University of Toronto says they graduate approximately 95 new dentists each year. What does this mean to you? It means there are a lot more new dentists than there were in the past. Yet, most patients prefer to entrust their dental care to an experienced dentist. Finding the right one from a list of hundreds can be a challenge. The following tips can help you connect with a professional who has stood the test of time in service as a practicing dentist.

 

Request Recommendations from Other Healthcare Providers

Ask your primary care doctor if they can recommend an experienced dentist in Toronto. In many cases, doctors collaborate for patients who need both medical and dental care. You can also ask your pharmacist, who likely fills prescriptions for dentists and may have connections in the dentistry profession. If you are preparing to move into Toronto or have just settled in here, you can call your former dentist and ask for his or her opinions.

 

Contact a Dental Association

The Canadian Dental Association is a well-respected organization you can turn to for information on local dentists. Dental associations, such as the Ontario Dental Association and the Royal College of Dental Surgeons of Ontario are also voluntary groups dentist may join. They can give you leads for finding an experienced dentist in Toronto.

  • Cda-adc.ca/en/
  • ca
  • on.ca/findadentist24
  • org

 

Search a Directory

Would you like a simple list of dentists? What about links to dental websites where you can read about the dentists’ experience and specializations? You can go to an online dental directory to find all this and more. Directories you can find online include:

  • com
  • ca
  • com/dentists/Toronto

 

Call on Your Personal Network for Help

Your personal network is made up of your family, your friends, business associates, and others you know in your personal, social and professional life. In some cases, they can be a reliable source of information on experienced dentists, especially if they live near you in Toronto. Ask for their advice and then do further research to make sure any dentist mentioned is right for you and your dental need

 

Now, Ask Questions!

A name is not enough to tell you what you need to know about the person who will become your dentist. You need to call or visit the dental office to find out more. Here are some good questions to ask:

  • How long have you been a practicing dentist?
  • Where were you educated and trained?
  • When did you graduate from dental school?
  • How often do you attend continuing educations courses?
  • Do you have any specialties or certifications beyond general dentistry?
  • What kind of dental care are you most experienced in doing?

 

The Reward Is a Dentist You Can Trust

In the end, finding a dentist is not the hardest thing most people have to do. It might take a bit of time, but be assured that it is well worth your effort. The result is that you find that dentist who knows dentistry inside and out and can understand and solve your dental care problems. An experienced dentist in Toronto is waiting for you.

    You want your teeth to look their whitest, brightest best. Yet, you do not have hours and hours of extra time on your hands, so it is perfectly understandable that you want to know how fast a teeth whitening procedure will take. The answer depends on what type of teeth whitening procedure you choose.

     

    In-Office Teeth Whitening

    Your dentist can whiten your teeth in the dental office in a fraction of the time it would take to do it in other ways. Your dentist can do this procedure in one office visit. After cleaning and checking your teeth and gums, the dentist can complete the whitening procedure in about an hour.

    There is currently no way of whitening teeth that is as safe and fast as in-office whitening. The results can be amazing, too, whitening your teeth from 9 to 12 shades in one hour-long session.

    The dentist begins the procedure by placing a teeth retractor in your mouth to expose the same teeth that show when you smile. Then, they paint on a liquid rubber or hardening resin to protect your gums. Your mouth is ready for the whitening.

    First, the dentist applies whitening gel to your teeth. The whitening gel is potent, and is best used under your dentist’s care. Next, the dentist might shine an intense light on your teeth to activate the whitening gel. You wait for 15 minutes, listening to music or watching TV while the gel does its work. The dentist then suctions off the gel, checks your teeth, and reapplies the gel one or two more times for 15 minutes each.

     

    Dentist-Supervised At-Home Whitening

    Although in-office whitening procedures are proven to be the most effective and least time consuming, they also cost more than other types of whitening. There is one alternative for people who would like professional whitening without paying the in-office cost. You can do teeth whitening at home under the direction of your dentist.

    It takes longer to whiten your teeth at home than it takes for the dentist to do it in the dental office. Yet, many experts suggest that the long-term results are as good as or better than with in-office teeth whitening. You just have to wait longer to see the results.

    In addition to a teeth-whitening kit with custom made whitening trays, your doctor sends you home with detailed instructions on how to use the kit. You get teeth whitening gel that is less strong than the gel your dentist uses in the office. You get instructions for using the whitening trays and gel, including how long to keep the trays in your mouth. There are also other methods on How to  Whiten Yellow Teeth At Home.

    Each whitening session can take from 1 hour to overnight, depending on the strength of the gel in the kit. You continue with daily at-home whitening sessions for 1 to 3 weeks, based on your dentist’s advice.

    No matter which way you choose to whiten your teeth, it is important to get your dentist’s recommendations for teeth whitening products and how to use them. Your dentist is your ultimate guide to teeth whitening safety and success.

      The world can be a hectic place. You have business to take care of, family and friends to spend time with, and your favorite activities to enjoy. Yet, you know you need to take care of your oral health by seeing your dentist regularly. What is more, you might need special dental services like fillings, braces or teeth whitening. Here are some tips to help you fit dentist appointments into your busy days.

       

      Choose a Well-Organized Dental Office

      You can follow the best scheduling practices in the world, but it can never make up for a dental office that is unorganized or off-schedule every time you go in. To avoid this problem, you need to choose your dentist wisely. Go into the office before you schedule your first visit to see if everything seems to be running smoothly. Notice whether people are checked in immediately. If someone is checked in as you are making your first appointment, you might find out how long it takes them to be called back to the dentist’s office. Paying attention to these seemingly small details can have a huge impact on your schedule later on.

       

      Make the Appointment at a Convenient Time

      Unless you have an urgent dental situation, you should have some control over when to have the appointment. The office staff should give you some options for different days and times. Look at your schedule and choose a day with as few other commitments as possible.

      If you work during the day, you can either make an evening or weekend appointment, or you can choose a time when you are coming or going from work. For example, you might have a routine checkup at the start of your day or a root canal at the end of the day. That way, you don’t have to leave the office for your dental appointment and then return to work when it is over.

      Combine Errands

      Suppose your dentist appointment is on a day when you are busy with many responsibilities and chores to do. Your time is more flexible than if you were at work, but you still do not seem to have enough of it. One way you can fit a dentist appointment into a day like that is to do several chores while you are out. Choose errands that take you near the dentist’s office before your appointment. You can shop, pick up dry cleaning or drive the kids to after-school activities and then drive to the dental office without making a special trip.

       

      Take Care of Your Teeth Now to Avoid Visits Later

      The single most important thing you can do to limit your time in the dentist’s office is to take care of your teeth every day. Brush for at least two and a half minutes at least twice a day and rinse between meals. Floss daily as well. Make sure you go in for your routine appointments. Remember that it does not take long to go in for a cleaning, but major dental or gum surgery can take many hours or even most of the day.
