No matter how well or how often you brush your teeth, minuscule food debris, as well as plaque, eventually build up. Sooner or later, this debris tends to accumulate between your teeth, which cause discoloration as well as cavity buildup. Although it’s nearly impossible to remove them using your personal dental cleaning supplies, there’s still a way to achieve thorough cleaning.

What is dental cleaning?

Dental cleaning is a simple and painless process that cleanses your teeth and help restore its bright and healthy glow. Teeth cleaning or an oral prophylaxis also alleviates any chances of developing oral cancer, tooth loss, and gum disease.

What should I expect from a dental cleaning treatment?

Oral examination

A dental hygienist often performs most teeth cleanings. Before the procedure starts, he will first conduct a thorough oral examination of the patient. He uses a dental mirror to check around your teeth as well as your gum. This helps him trace for any oral and gum issues such as gingivitis. If he notices any problems, he might call the dentist to get an approval to proceed with the deep dental cleaning procedure.

Tartar and plaque removal

Some use regular teeth cleaning kits to remove the food debris stuck in the mouth. However, that’s not enough to complete the job. Soon, plaque and tartar will develop, causing teeth imperfection and even deterioration. The dental hygienist uses a periodontal scaler to eliminate both the tartar and plaque around the patient’s gum line. He uses the dental mirror with it to guide him in cleaning. He will also use both to clean all those hard to reach areas between your teeth. It’s an essential part of the dental cleaning process to eliminate most of the plaque and tartar in your teeth.

Toothpaste cleaning, flossing, and rinsing

Once your teeth are completely free of plaque and tartar, the hygienist then uses a high-powered electric brush to deeply clean your teeth. He’ll also use the device to remove any remaining tartar left behind. He will also use a toothpaste that has a powdery consistency to scrub your teeth gently. The professional teeth cleaning procedure is safe to be done twice a year. However, attempting to do it at home without a professional’s supervision may cause your teeth’s enamel to wear down.

Next, the dental hygienist will floss your teeth to get deep between your teeth. Doing so will help remove any plaque or toothpaste that remained from the previous deep dental cleaning procedure. Lastly, he will give you a rinse that has liquid fluoride to help get rid of any debris in your mouth.

Teeth polishing

The dental hygienist uses a slow-speed dental handpiece to polish your teeth and bring back its natural shine. This procedure will also help return your teeth’s original smoothness once the dental cleaning procedure is complete.

Fluoride treatment

The dental hygienist uses various fluoride applications to strengthen your teeth’s enamel. It’ll also help protect your teeth against cavities for several months. These fluoride treatments may come in either liquid, foam, or gel foam. Before the dental hygienist starts the procedure, he’ll ask you what flavor you prefer. Then, he’ll apply the fluoride treatment to a mouthpiece and places it over your teeth for a minute. The dental hygienist also paints fluoride varnish onto your teeth using a small brush. The fluoride varnish will solidify as soon as it comes to contact with saliva. Once it’s completely hardened, you may immediately eat and drink after the procedure.

These are just a few of the things that you may expect from a regular deep dental cleaning procedure. It’s best to visit your dentist for regular dental cleaning services in London, Ontario to help maintain your oral health. It will make you feel more at ease and comfortable with your oral health and hygiene.
