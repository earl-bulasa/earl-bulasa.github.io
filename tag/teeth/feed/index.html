Invisible braces not only allow you to look your best as you have your teeth straightened, but they are also more convenient than traditional braces. You can go about your life as usual, with the least amount of alteration in your schedule and daily activities as possible. At the same time, your teeth are getting straighter and more attractive with each passing day.

 

Invisible Braces Make Going out a Breeze

Are you going out to an important event where you want to look your best? Invisible braces are perfect for special occasions. You have the choice of taking them out during the event and popping them right back in when you get home. Or, you can simply keep them in the whole time. No one will ever know you are wearing them!

 

You Eat What You Want

Whether you are going out for a dinner with friends or eating in the comfort of your own home, it is nice to be able to eat the foods you like most. With traditional braces, you might have trouble eating certain foods that are hard, sticky or even many raw fruits and vegetables. With invisible braces, you can eat what you like and you can eat the healthiest foods. If you have any difficulty with the braces in, you can simply remove them while you eat and put them back in when you finish.

 

You Can Follow a Normal Daily Dental Care Routine

Taking care of your daily dental hygiene is a real challenge when you have traditional braces. It can take up to a half hour just to do the brushing. Even then, it is hard to be sure you have cleaned them perfectly. With invisible braces, you can take them out for cleaning. When you do, you can brush and floss normally, just as you did before you had them.

 

You Are More Comfortable with Invisible Braces

Traditional braces can be uncomfortable to wear. They can even cut the inside of your mouth if they are not adjusted properly. Invisible braces fit comfortably in your mouth. The reason they are so special is they are custom made for your teeth. The dentist takes impressions of your mouth and the aligners are made to fit over your teeth without any pain or significant discomfort. You can spend more time enjoying your life and less time thinking about your braces.

 

You Need Fewer Trips to the Dentist

If you have a busy schedule, you are going to like invisible braces for another reason. That is, you have to take fewer trips to the dentist with invisible braces. Unless you have an unexpected problem, you can usually go six weeks between visits to the dentist for checkups and adjustments. This frees your valuable time to do what you need to do and what you like to do best. With invisible braces, you can go about your business just as you like. It is so easy to adapt to them that you can forget them and do just what you like to do.

    You want healthier gums and teeth, but you do not want to use synthetic commercially made products. What do you do? It turns out that you can take care of your oral health naturally without resorting to spending a lot of money. Here are seven ways to get the healthy pink gums and clean teeth you want.

    1. Brush Vigorously

    For this tip, you do not need anything more than what you already have – a toothbrush. Now that fancy toothpastes are available everywhere, it is easy to rely on them to do the work for you. However, if you take the time to clean your teeth properly, it matters a lot less what you use to clean them. Brush vigorously for at least two and a half minutes before you put down your toothbrush.

    2. Make Your Own Toothpaste

    If you do not want to use commercial toothpastes, you can always make your own using simple ingredients you can find on your grocery store shelves. Here are a few simple ideas for homemade toothpastes.

    • Baking soda, sea salt, essential oil and water.
    • Coconut oil and baking soda.
    • Vegan toothpaste – vegetable glycerin, guar gum, baking soda, essential oil and water

    3. Use Essential Oils

    Research shows that using specific essential oils can improve your oral health in several ways. To use them, you can add them to toothpaste or mouthwash you make at home. Here are five of the most beneficial.

    • Peppermint oil – kills bacteria that thrive in the low-oxygen environment of your mouth.
    • Myrrh oil – heals mouth sores, soothes gums and increases the blood flow to your gums and mouth tissues to keep them healthy.
    • Cinnamon oil – fights tooth decay and gum disease.
    • Spearmint oil – antiseptic that can soothe your gums and destroy germs.
    • Clove oil – soothes, heals and relieves pain in the teeth and gums.

    4. Drink and Swish with Green Tea

    You might have already heard that drinking green tea can help you lose weight. Did you also know that it works well to improve your oral health? It helps prevent your teeth from eroding and keeps starchy foods from decaying your teeth. Swish with it like mouthwash or simply drink more of it.

    5. Take Vitamin D

    Taking an ordinary Vitamin D capsule can prevent gum disease and kill bacteria in your mouth. If you prefer to get your vitamins the natural way, you can eat foods rich in Vitamin D such as salmon, tuna, Portobello mushrooms, egg yolks or beef liver. You can also get your daily dose of Vitamin D by taking a spoonful of cod liver oil. And do not forget to get out into the sunshine as often as possible.

    6. Eat More Pineapple

    Pineapple has an enzyme in it called bromelain. This enzyme removes stains from your teeth. You can also whiten your teeth considerably by eating plenty of crunchy fruits and vegetables.

    7. Try Oil Pulling

    Oil pulling is a practice that comes from ancient times. All you need to do is wait until morning, before you have eaten or drunk anything. Measure out a tablespoon of sunflower or sesame oil and place it in your mouth. Swish it around for 15 minutes. As the oil moves through the vacuum of your mouth, it gets deep around the roots of your teeth to remove food particles and bacteria.

      You want your teeth to look their whitest, brightest best. Yet, you do not have hours and hours of extra time on your hands, so it is perfectly understandable that you want to know how fast a teeth whitening procedure will take. The answer depends on what type of teeth whitening procedure you choose.

       

      In-Office Teeth Whitening

      Your dentist can whiten your teeth in the dental office in a fraction of the time it would take to do it in other ways. Your dentist can do this procedure in one office visit. After cleaning and checking your teeth and gums, the dentist can complete the whitening procedure in about an hour.

      There is currently no way of whitening teeth that is as safe and fast as in-office whitening. The results can be amazing, too, whitening your teeth from 9 to 12 shades in one hour-long session.

      The dentist begins the procedure by placing a teeth retractor in your mouth to expose the same teeth that show when you smile. Then, they paint on a liquid rubber or hardening resin to protect your gums. Your mouth is ready for the whitening.

      First, the dentist applies whitening gel to your teeth. The whitening gel is potent, and is best used under your dentist’s care. Next, the dentist might shine an intense light on your teeth to activate the whitening gel. You wait for 15 minutes, listening to music or watching TV while the gel does its work. The dentist then suctions off the gel, checks your teeth, and reapplies the gel one or two more times for 15 minutes each.

       

      Dentist-Supervised At-Home Whitening

      Although in-office whitening procedures are proven to be the most effective and least time consuming, they also cost more than other types of whitening. There is one alternative for people who would like professional whitening without paying the in-office cost. You can do teeth whitening at home under the direction of your dentist.

      It takes longer to whiten your teeth at home than it takes for the dentist to do it in the dental office. Yet, many experts suggest that the long-term results are as good as or better than with in-office teeth whitening. You just have to wait longer to see the results.

      In addition to a teeth-whitening kit with custom made whitening trays, your doctor sends you home with detailed instructions on how to use the kit. You get teeth whitening gel that is less strong than the gel your dentist uses in the office. You get instructions for using the whitening trays and gel, including how long to keep the trays in your mouth. There are also other methods on How to  Whiten Yellow Teeth At Home.

      Each whitening session can take from 1 hour to overnight, depending on the strength of the gel in the kit. You continue with daily at-home whitening sessions for 1 to 3 weeks, based on your dentist’s advice.

      No matter which way you choose to whiten your teeth, it is important to get your dentist’s recommendations for teeth whitening products and how to use them. Your dentist is your ultimate guide to teeth whitening safety and success.
