How can sedation dentistry in Toronto help you overcome your fear of the dentist or procedures? To answer this first we must look at the safety of sedation dentistry.

 

Safety and types of sedation

In order to understand how safe sedation dentistry is you need to know that only trained and licensed sedation dentists can practice sedation dentistry so not all dentists can do sedation. Before you are given any sedation with sedation dentistry a detailed medical history will be taken and you will need to provide a list of any medications you are currently taking and why. While you are sedated your heart rate and breathing will be monitored.

 

Types of sedation

  • Oral in which pills or liquid such as valium to help you relax will be used
  • Inhalation where you will be giving nitrous oxide better known as laughing gas
  • IV moderate sedation where you will be given a sedative through a vein
  • General anesthesia which is where you are given medication that relaxes you and puts you to sleep

With any of these, you will also generally be given a local anesthesia of Novocain to numb the area of the mouth that is being worked on.

 

How sedation helps you

Sedation can provide help to those who are extremely fearful of going to the dentist or are anxious when going to the dentist to have procedures done. It also helps young children receive proper dental care without being further traumatized by the dentist because the sedation helps relieve the fear and anxiousness that the child has. In effect, it is a great tool for both children and adults that allow the patient to have dental care procedures done in order for your Toronto Ontario Dentist to keep their mouth and teeth health.

    Oral surgery can be a serious undertaking, but there are several conditions that merit a surgical procedure. If you know what to expect from your procedure, it can help you be more prepared for it. Here are a few common types of oral surgeries and what you should expect.

     

    Removing Impacted Wisdom Teeth

    The last teeth to develop are the third molars. Sometimes, these teeth do not cause issues, but for many people they do not fully erupt, or they are improperly aligned. They can become impacted between the jaw bone and the gums. This can have a negative effect on the health of adjacent and surrounding teeth.

     

    Dental Implants

    Implants are one of the most modern ways to replace missing teeth. A metal post is surgically implanted into the jawbone. Over time, it will fuse with the bone and the gum tissue. An artificial, but real-looking tooth is placed over the post once the site has healed. The healing process can vary but can take weeks to months to heal completely.

    Tooth Extraction

    Having a tooth extracted is the most common type of oral surgery. Alternative treatment options such a root canals or tooth restoration is the treatment of choice, but an extraction may be necessary for many people.

     

    Jaw Surgery

    If the jaws are not aligned properly, it can cause problems with your appearance and with the function of the mouth. Surgery is necessary in many cases to correct bites or restore full function.

     

    Before Oral Surgery

    Your dental professional will offer you pre-surgery treatment plans. They will discuss appropriate sedation methods and offer you post-surgery treatment plans.

     

    After Oral Surgery

    There is always a recovery period after any type of surgery. If you are placed under general anesthesia, you will need someone to drive you home. Your dentist will provide you with specific post-surgery instructions for you to follow.

      When it comes to our families, we want to find the best Toronto sedation dentistry for kids. If you are not sure about sedation techniques or are unsure what you are looking for, it can make for a difficult decision. Worst case, you may take your child to a dentist who isn’t compatible with your child. If you know what to look for, you can find the best Toronto sedation dentistry for kids.

      What is Toronto Sedation Dentistry for Kids?

      A pediatric dentist, or one who works well with children, will be up to date on the most modern forms of sedation used for kids today. Based on the child’s needs and anxiety level they may offer either nitrous oxide or an oral sedation for a child.

       

      What is nitrous oxide?

      Nitrous oxide, or laughing gas, is the most commonly used Toronto sedation dentistry for kids. It is administered via a small mask which is placed over the child’s nose. It will help the child relax, but it won’t put them to sleep. The mild gas is not addictive, is administered easily and the body eliminates it quickly as soon as normal breathing is resumed. The child will remain fully conscious and has all their natural reflexes.

       

      What is oral sedation for kids?

      Oral sedation is used for children who deal with above average apprehension about visiting the dentist. It may also be used for children with special needs. It will help reduce their anxiety as well as any discomfort. Oral sedation will make the child sleepy, but they do not lose consciousness at any time.

       

      What Parents Should Know

      Parents should discuss Toronto sedation dentistry for kids with their dental professional prior to procedures. The dentist should be informed of any changes in the child’s medical condition, respiratory conditions that make breathing difficult or if they are on any medications.
